Layer 1 GTM Diagnosis — Rivia
Use Case · One Sentence
Rivia.AI enables B2B SaaS teams to create full-fidelity, interactive product demos in minutes using HTML capture, so prospects can experience the product without live handholding — replacing static screenshots, recorded videos, and expensive live demo calls.
Job To Be Done

When a B2B SaaS company wants to let prospects experience their product before buying — without engineering effort, without scheduling a live call, and without the limitations of video walkthroughs — they need a way to create realistic, interactive, shareable product demos that marketing can embed on websites, sales can personalize for deals, and CS can use for onboarding.

Value Thesis

Rivia.AI's value thesis is that in a crowded interactive demo market dominated by well-funded players pricing at $500+/mo for teams, there is a massive underserved segment of early-stage and growth-stage SaaS companies that need the same capability at 10x lower cost with 10x faster time-to-value. By combining aggressive pricing ($40/mo entry), genuine speed (10-minute setup via Chrome extension), high-fidelity HTML capture, and a unique mobile app demo capability, Rivia can win the long tail of the market that Walnut, Demostack, and Reprise will never serve — and grow with those companies as they scale. The risk is that better-funded competitors like Storylane and Arcade are attacking the same segment with similar pricing and more capital, making Rivia's window to establish defensible positioning narrow and shrinking.

Paulina’s 90-Day Plan
Rivia.AI is an early-stage, likely bootstrapped interactive demo platform with genuine product differentiation (mobile demos, HTML fidelity, speed, price) but an under-built GTM engine competing in a category being consolidated by players with 10-50x more capital. The 90-day plan must focus on capital-efficient moves that create defensible positioning before the funding gap becomes fatal. The core strategic question is: own a niche decisively or get squeezed out of a crowded middle.
P1
Commit to a PLG motion with a true free tier and kill the ambiguity
Rivia's stated values, price point, and product (Chrome extension, 10-minute setup) scream PLG, but they're running a sales-assisted motion with 'Book a Demo' CTAs. At their team size and funding level, they cannot out-sell Walnut or Demostack. They CAN out-PLG them. Launch a free tier (e.g., 1 user, 3 demos, Rivia watermark) to generate volume, capture emails, and create a self-serve funnel. Move the $40 Starter to a Pro tier. Use the Growth/Enterprise tiers for expansion revenue only after users have already activated. This aligns their stated philosophy with their actual motion and plays to their capital-efficiency advantage.
P2
Build and publish 5 case studies with quantified ROI in 30 days
100+ customers and zero case studies is the single highest-leverage gap to close. Pick the 5 best customers (prioritize any with recognizable names or strong metrics), interview them, and publish case studies with specific numbers: conversion rate lift, demos created per month, time saved vs. video/live demos, pipeline influenced. These assets unlock every other GTM channel — they power the website, fuel outbound, arm champions for internal buy-in, and dramatically improve close rates on Growth/Enterprise deals.
P3
Own the 'mobile app demos' niche with a dedicated landing page, content cluster, and targeted outbound
Customer testimonial explicitly claims Rivia is 'the only product on the market that supports demos for mobile apps.' If true, this is a category-of-one positioning opportunity. Create a dedicated /mobile-demos landing page, write 5-7 SEO articles targeting 'interactive demo for mobile apps,' 'mobile app product tour software,' etc. Run targeted outbound to mobile-first SaaS companies, fintech apps, and consumer tech with product-market fit messaging. This is a defensible wedge that well-funded horizontal competitors are unlikely to prioritize.
