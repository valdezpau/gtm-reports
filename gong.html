Layer 1 GTM Diagnosis — Gong
Use Case · One Sentence
Gong captures every customer interaction across calls, emails, and meetings, then uses AI to surface insights, automate workflows, and power revenue teams to forecast accurately, coach effectively, and close more deals.
Job To Be Done

When revenue leaders need to understand what's actually happening in their pipeline — beyond what reps self-report in the CRM — they need a system that captures ground truth from customer interactions and turns it into actionable intelligence that drives consistent execution across the entire GTM org.

Value Thesis

Gong is the only Revenue AI Operating System that unifies the complete customer interaction record — across every channel, every deal stage, and every team — into a proprietary Revenue Graph that powers AI agents, insights, and workflows purpose-built for revenue teams. In a market where CRM vendors and communication platforms are bolting on AI features as afterthoughts, Gong's moat is the cross-platform, interaction-level data layer trained on billions of real customer conversations. The strategic bet is that the company that owns the 'revenue graph' — the ground truth of what customers actually say, feel, and do — will become the system of record for revenue execution, displacing both legacy CRM reporting and fragmented point solutions. At 5,000+ customers and a $7.25B valuation, the question is no longer product-market fit — it's whether Gong can convert its conversation intelligence dominance into a multi-product platform before incumbents close the gap with native AI.

Paulina’s 90-Day Plan
Gong is a category-defining, late-stage company (~$7.25B valuation) at a critical inflection point: transitioning from 'conversation intelligence leader' to 'Revenue AI OS platform.' The GTM challenge is not demand generation or brand awareness — Gong has those in spades. The real challenge is executing a multi-product platform expansion while defending against both incumbents bundling AI natively and point solutions nibbling at the edges. The 90-day plan should focus on accelerating platform attach rates, sharpening the enterprise expansion playbook, and de-risking the transition from single-product hero to multi-product platform.
P1
Launch a 'Revenue AI OS Maturity Assessment' — a structured sales methodology and supporting content framework that segments prospects/customers into tiers (Point Solution → Platform → AI OS) and maps each to a specific expansion path with clear ROI milestones. Equip every AE and CSM with this assessment.
The biggest GTM risk right now is that 80%+ of Gong's 5,000 customers still think of Gong as 'the call recording tool.' Expanding wallet share from Engage → Forecast → Enable → Agents requires a consultative, land-and-expand methodology that ties each product to a measurable business outcome. The newly hired Revenue Architects team and the Customer Office restructure signal Gong knows this — but the field needs a codified playbook, not just a new org chart.
P2
Build a 'Gong vs. Native AI' competitive battle card and displacement program specifically targeting the Microsoft Copilot for Sales and Salesforce Einstein narratives. Position Gong as the 'cross-platform revenue graph' that sits above any single CRM or communication tool.
The #1 objection in enterprise deals is now 'Why do I need Gong when my CRM/Teams/Zoom is adding AI?' Gong's moat is the Revenue Graph — the cross-platform, cross-channel data layer that no single vendor can replicate. But if this narrative isn't crisp, specific, and armed with quantified proof points, it will be lost in procurement committees. This needs to be the core competitive positioning for the next 12 months.
P3
Introduce a 'Gong Essentials' tier with published pricing for teams of 10–50 — a simplified, self-serve entry point that includes core conversation intelligence + basic AI summaries, designed to feed the enterprise pipeline from below.
Gong's most dangerous long-term threat isn't Clari or Salesloft — it's the generation of sales leaders who start their careers using free/cheap tools like Fathom, Fireflies, or native Zoom AI. If Gong doesn't own the 'first tool a sales team buys' motion, it risks becoming a 'tool large enterprises switch to' rather than a 'platform teams grow up with.' A transparent, accessible entry tier would also dramatically reduce CAC for the lower mid-market segment.

Every feature — Enable for coaching, Forecast for new deal types, Engage for multi-threaded outreach, Agents for automation — maps directly to a specific IAM selling challenge. The discovery should surface the gap between Docusign's IAM ambition and their current ability to measure and drive rep behavior change at scale. That gap is the urgency.
